movie: 12 Angry Men (1957)
==========================

:: Title
12 Angry Men

:: Date
1957-04-10

:: Description
12 Angry Men is a 1957 American courtroom drama film directed by Sidney Lumet,
adapted from a teleplay of the same name by Reginald Rose. This courtroom
drama tells the story of a jury of 12 men as they deliberate the conviction or
acquittal of an 18-year old defendant on the basis of reasonable doubt,
forcing the jurors to question their morals and values.

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/12_Angry_Men_(1957_film)

:: Ratings
5 <mikael>

:: Choice Themes
guilt and evidence [the story centred on whether certain evidence was enough to support a verdict of guilty beyond reasonable doubt]
sacrifice for one's beliefs [especilly juror 9 stood up alone against immense peer pressure, for his belief in justice]

:: Major Themes
working together in spite of disagreement [the 12 men were disagreeing angrily and often ended up insulting each other and havong to swallow insults in order to progress]
capital punishment [The story kept coming back to the fact that the boy would be sent to the electric chair.]
patricide [A boy was alleged to have killed his father.]
domestic violence [the story centred around a case of domestic abuse (the father had hit the boy) which allegedly culminated in murder]
the presumption of innocence [The argument frequently came back to the point that it was the accuser's burden to prove guilt.]
the inclination to believe what one wants to believe [the jury concluded that the two witnesses had in fact false convinced themelves that they saw and heard things that they most likely never did]
the desire for justice [many people wanted passionately to convict a man they thought was a killer]

:: Minor Themes
manners [some of the men were rude to others and occasionally got called out about it]
poverty in society [the squalor of the neighborhood the boy came from was pointed out]
racism in society [Juror 7 had racial slurs for Juror 11, and there were other more or less racist remarks]
standing up to a bully [especially juror 3 tried to bully people into agreeing with him, but juror 9 stood up to him (and others)]
prejudicial attitude of superiority against someone less well off than one's self [Some of the jurors displayed an irrational belief in the boy's guilt, apparently supported by the fact that he was poor and abused.]


movie: Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (1937)
=============================================

:: Title
Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs

:: Date
1937-12-21

:: Description
Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs is a 1937 American animated musical fantasy
film produced by Walt Disney Productions and originally released by RKO Radio
Pictures. Based on the 1812 German fairy tale by the Brothers Grimm, it is the
first full-length cel animated feature film and the earliest Disney animated
feature film. The story was adapted by storyboard artists Dorothy Ann Blank,
Richard Creedon, Merrill De Maris, Otto Englander, Earl Hurd, Dick Rickard,
Ted Sears and Webb Smith. David Hand was the supervising director, while
William Cottrell, Wilfred Jackson, Larry Morey, Perce Pearce, and Ben
Sharpsteen directed the film's individual sequences.

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow_White_and_the_Seven_Dwarfs_(1937_film)

:: Ratings
5 <mikael>

:: Choice Themes
female-female rivalry [the Queen was in competition with Snow White for the position of most beautiful]
wicked character vs. virtuous character [th queen was clearly evil; Snow White was clearly good hearted]

:: Major Themes
female vanity [the queen was vain to the point of murder]
jealousy [the queen was envious of Snow White's beauty]
the desire to be beautiful [the queen wanted o be the most beautiful in the land]
epic love [the prince's legendary love made him seek out Snow White even after she had died]
what if a killer was after me [Snow White had to run away because the queen was out for her life]
the battle of the sexes [the feminine ideal Snow White was contrasted with the stereotypically male dwarfs, especially with regard to cleanliness]

:: Minor Themes
coping with being lost in the forest [Snow White got herself more or less lost in the forest ere she stumbled upon the dwarfs' house]
greed for riches [The Seven Dwarfs dug greedily for jewels all day long.]
magic potion [the wicked queen brewed up vile potions]
sloppy character vs. tidy character [Snow White was tidy; the dwarfs were sloppy]
duty vs. conscience [the huntsman could not bring himself to slaughter Snow White as the queen had ordered him to]

movie: The Lady Vanishes (1938)
===============================

:: Title
The Lady Vanishes

:: Date
1938-10-07

:: Description
The Lady Vanishes is a 1938 British mystery thriller film directed by Alfred
Hitchcock, starring Margaret Lockwood and Michael Redgrave. Written by Sidney
Gilliat and Frank Launder based on the 1936 novel The Wheel Spins by Ethel
Lina White, the film is about a beautiful English tourist travelling by train
in continental Europe who discovers that her elderly travelling companion
seems to have disappeared from the train. After her fellow passengers deny
ever having seen the elderly lady, the young woman is helped by a young
musicologist, the two proceeding to search the train for clues to the old
lady's disappearance.

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lady_Vanishes

:: Ratings
4 <mikael>

:: Major Themes
communicating with someone who speaks a different language [there were recurring instances of Anglophones trying to communicate with continentals who, outrageously, did not understand English]
foreign point of view [a variety of British people were commenting on what they found on the continent]
what if I told the truth and nobody would believe me [Iris became upset when no one she interviewed would admit to having seen her with Miss Froy]
what if a killer was after me [it transpires that evil people were in fact after Miss Froy, and then everyone, with murderous intent]
secret agent occupation [It transpires that Miss Froy was a spy, and everything that happened around her was connected to that fact.]
infatuation [especially Gilbert and Iris became besotted and decided to marry in the end]

:: Minor Themes
conflict of privacy preferences [two Britih gentlemen were greatly flustered when a hotel maid began undressing in front of their eyes]
corruption in society [Iris bribed a hotel manager]
greed for riches [The hotel manager's eyes shone with greed as he catered to Iris and her friends.]
cricket [Charters and Caldicott were obsessed with the game of cricket.]
extramarital affair [Todhunter's mistress pressed him about when he'd divorce his wife; we heard that she was married herself]
hallucination of a non-existing person [we must ponder the possibility that the knock on the head made Iris hallucinate a woman named Miss Froy]
espionage [Miss Froy was a spy with an important message for Britain's foreign office]


movie: The Night of the Hunter (1955)
=====================================

:: Title
The Night of the Hunter

:: Date
1955-07-26

:: Description
The Night of the Hunter is a 1955 American thriller film directed by Charles
Laughton, and starring Robert Mitchum, Shelley Winters, and Lillian Gish. The
screenplay by James Agee was based on the 1953 novel of the same title by
Davis Grubb. The plot focuses on a corrupt minister-turned-serial killer who
attempts to charm an unsuspecting widow and steal $10,000 hidden by her
executed husband.

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Night_of_the_Hunter_(film)

:: Ratings
4 <mikael> [stilted characters and rambling ending]

evil can come in the guise of good [It seemed the point of the story was to caution that the vilest of people may come in the guise of the good.]
be wary of strangers [It seemed the point of the story was to caution that the vilest of people may come in the guise of the good.]
good vs. evil [The reverend pretending to be good showed himself as evil and was contrasted with the exceptionally benign and responsible Rachel Cooper, who defeated him soundly at the end.]

:: Major Themes
brother and sister [John and Pearl were at the center of the story]
murder [Ben was hanged for murder. Harry killed numerous people in his hunt for the money.]
husband and wife [Willa and Ben indirectly; Willa and Harry]
religious fundamentalism [Harry Powell was a self proclaimed preacher of the fire-and-brimstone variety. It was later revealed that this was all facade the better to lure his gullible, pious victims in and part them from their money.]
Christianity [a self-proclaimed reverend was the main antagonist and kept preaching fanatically]
uncle and nephew [Uncle Birdie and John]
keeping secrets [the kids struggled to keep the money secret from Harry]
greed for riches [Harry was particular was covetous of the $10,000.]
chastity [Reverend Harry refused to make love to his new wife and complained about lasciviousness in general]
